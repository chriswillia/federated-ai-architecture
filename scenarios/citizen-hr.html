Citizen-built HR Benefits Agent

A business analyst — not a developer — ships an enterprise-grade agent in Teams.

By the end of this flow, an HR specialist gets a vetted, source-grounded benefits answer in under a minute — with one identity, one cost owner, and full lineage from question to answer.

An HR business analyst builds a benefits Q&A agent in Copilot Studio. Employees ask it in M365 Copilot (Teams). The agent grounds answers in HR knowledge through Foundry IQ, and only reaches into Microsoft Graph or the on-prem HRIS when it needs personal data — under one identity, one cost owner, and full lineage.

What success looks like

Considerations

Citizen-developer enablement
A make-vs-buy decision sits next to a help-vs-supervise one. Plan templates, guardrails, and an approval workflow before publishing.
Curated MCP server catalog
Citizen developers should not be picking arbitrary MCP servers. Curate an approved set with signed manifests.
PII handling discipline
Reads from HRIS and Graph mean personal data is in scope. Set classification, retention, and DLP policy in Purview up front.
HRIS connector security
On-prem HRIS access usually means a service principal or a private endpoint. Decide who rotates secrets and who can connect.
