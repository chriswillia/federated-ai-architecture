Customer conversation flow

Central governance. Decentralized innovation.

A federated AI architecture — foundational controls centralized, agent development decentralized — so the business moves fast and the enterprise stays in charge.

Why federated?

Today, AI capabilities and data are fragmented across Azure, AWS, GCP, SaaS, and on-prem — each with its own identities, tools, and silos, driving inconsistent governance and inefficiency. A federated AI architecture unifies a single control plane for identity, governance, and observability across clouds, while domain teams stay free to build on the platforms best suited to their work — combining central trust and compliance with local agility.

For the CISO conversation

Trust your AI estate.

See every agent. Govern every prompt. Catch every misuse. A federated AI architecture extends your existing Microsoft Sentinel, Defender, Purview, and Entra controls to AI — including the AI your employees use without telling you.

Why this matters now

Shadow AI is the new shadow IT. Without an AI-aware control plane, prompt-injection, data exfiltration, and excessive agency all bypass your existing tooling. The same identity, DLP, and SIEM that protect your data today need to see agents, tools, and tokens — not just users and files.

For the CIO & CTO conversation

One platform. Many clouds. Zero rewrites.

A federated AI architecture lets you build once and deploy anywhere — Azure, AWS, GCP, on-prem, or inside SAP and Salesforce — using MCP, A2A, and APIM as the shared wiring, with Foundry as the central control plane.

Why federated?

Most enterprises have AI capabilities and data spread across multiple clouds and SaaS apps. Picking one cloud means starving the others. A federated model gives every team local agility on the runtime that fits the job, while identity, governance, and observability stay unified.

For the AI & business leader conversation

AI in your daily work, governed by default.

Employees use M365 Copilot, business-app copilots, and citizen-built agents in Copilot Studio — one chat surface, every business app, every backend system — without IT chasing shadow tools or rebuilding integrations per department.

Why this matters now

The fastest-growing AI use cases aren't pro-code apps — they're employee-built agents that answer HR questions, file expenses, summarize tickets. The win is not another tool; it's one consistent surface for users and one consistent control plane for IT.
